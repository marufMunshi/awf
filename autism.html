What is Autism

Autism

Autism is a complex development disability that typically appears during the first three years of life and affects a person’s ability to communication and interacts with others. [Autism is spectrum disorder, which means the condition has wide ranging degrees of severity]There is also a condition called Asperser Syndrome which is a form of autism used to describe people at the higher functioning end of the Autism Spectrum.

Characteristics of Autism

The degree or characteristics differ from person to person, but all those affected have impairment in Social interaction, social communication, repetitive and restricted patterns of behavior. This is referred to as a triad of impairment.
Social Communication (Difficulty with verbal and non-verbal communication)
Social Interaction (Difficulty with social relationship)
Behavior (Repetitive and restricted patterns of behavior)
In addition to this triad, resistance to change in routine is a notable feature Individual with autism may also experiences sensivity in the senses of sight, hearing, touch, smell, movement.

Causes of Autism

Autism is a brain disorder which affects the way brain uses information. The exact cause or causes of autism is/are still unknown. Research shows that genetic factors are important. Autism may indeed result from a combination of several ‘’causes’’. [Autism is a brain based development disorder. No factors in the psychological environment of the Child cause autism]

Autism and Mental Retardation

Most people with mental retardation show relatively even skill development, while individuals with autism typically show uneven skill development with deficits in certain areas-most frequently in their ability to communicate and relate to others.It is important to distinguish autism from mental retardation or other disorder since diagnostic confusion may result in referral inappropriate and ineffective treatment techniques.

Autism is Treatable

Studies show that people who have autism can improve significantly with proper education. Early diagnosis and intensive behavioral intervention can have a significant and lasting positive impact on Autistic children. Autistic children can benefit from placement in a good educational program. Through specially trained teachers, using specially structured programs that emphasize individual instruction, persons with autism can learn to function at home and in the community.

If a child shows any of these signs, please ask your pediatrician or family practitioner for an immediate evaluation:

  • No big smile or other warm, joyful experiences by 6 months.
  • No back and forth sharing of sounds, smiles or other facial expression by 9 months.
  • No babbling by 12 months.
  • No gestures (point, wave, grasp) by 12 months.
  • No single word by 16 months.
  • No two words meaningful phrases (without imitating or repeating) by 24 months.
  • Any loss of speech or babbling or social skills at any age People with autism are not physically disabled; they look just like anybody without disability. Due to this invisible nature it is much harder to create awareness and understanding of the Condition.
